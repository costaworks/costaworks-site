About

The engineer
doing the work.

I'm a mechanical engineer who has worked across the full product lifecycle — from early concept through production. My background is rooted in the automotive aftermarket, where designs have to be durable, cost-conscious, and buildable in the real world.

Background

Built in the real world.

My work has shipped on professional race cars, street vehicles around the world, and in products used by Fortune 500 companies. It has also included custom hardware, audio systems, and manufacturing tooling.

The automotive aftermarket is an unforgiving environment. Parts have to survive real abuse, pass muster with demanding customers, and be manufacturable at a price that makes sense. That mindset carries into every project I take on, regardless of industry.

Over the years I've been involved at nearly every stage of product development — not just the design phase. That means I design with manufacturing, tolerances, inspection, and cost already in mind. Fewer surprises later.

Philosophy

Why I work independently.

"The people doing the work should be talking to the people with the problem."

I've worked with companies of many sizes. Larger firms have their place, but they often create distance between clients and engineers — communication slows, accountability diffuses, and the person with context isn't the one in the meeting.

Working independently keeps it simple. You talk to me. I do the work. If a project scales beyond what I can handle solo, I have a trusted network of experienced engineers I can bring in — without sacrificing accountability.
